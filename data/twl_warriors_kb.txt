### FULLBACK

Charnze Nicoll-Klokstad
or ball-playing livewire
Taine Tuaupiki

Both had blinders as the also-ran Warriors rallied from 18 points down to beat Cronulla on the bell at Sharks Stadium. Earlier in the season, Tuaupiki was the late-try-and-sideline-conversion hero of the Warriors’ unforgettable Magic Round upset of Penrith, with Nicoll-Klokstad playing a strong hand at five-eighth.

### CENTRES & WINGERS
Imagine saying in any other previous pre-season that the only player locked into a definitive Warriors three-quarter spot is
Adam Pompey
But that reality for 2026 is a reflection of the long-serving clubman’s career-best campaign last year, along with the uncertainty surrounding the top combination of the Warriors’ plentiful wingers and centre options.
Firstly, let’s celebrate 120-game Pompey’s progress. The enigmatic 27-year-old played all 25 games last season (22 at centre, three on the wing) and scored 11 tries – more than double his previous best return – and chalked up seven try assists, as well averaging a tick under 100 metres per game.
‘Wets’ is virtually a certainty to line up in the centres in 2026.
Roger Tuivasa-Sheck
would seem to be a guaranteed left wing pick after scooping the Simon Mannering Medal and the Warriors’ Players’ Player award in 2025, grabbing a career-high-equalling 12 tries in 19 games, and dominating the NRL’s run metres categories.

### HALVES
For all the current Warriors squad’s virtues under Andrew Webster and the club’s trajectory, their 2026 NRL campaign seems to hinge precariously on the health of a player with just 15 starts in the No.7 jersey who won’t be available until the second half of April.
Luke Metcalf
was bullish about wanting to be the player to step into Shaun Johnson’s shoes last season, despite never having lined up at halfback in the NRL – and he ultimately lived up to his own hype as one of the individual stars of the first four months of the 2025 season.
Following an encouraging but patchy start, the fleet-footed Metcalf – who had been restricted to just seven top-grade appearances the previous season – found his groove in the high-pressure position and was integral to the Warriors’ historic 10-3 start.
Metcalf’s early standout moments predominantly stemmed from his support play and speed, but he gradually became more confident in taking on the line and making things happen, compiling a mouth-watering highlights reel. His ball-playing and kicking game was serviceable, though far from elite.

### HOOKER
The No.9 jersey is one of the most settled in the Warriors’ line-up, with Wayde Egan widely rated – when at his best, at least – as a top-five NRL hooker or higher.
But how coach Andrew Webster utilises freewheeling understudy Sam Healey’s game-breaking talents shapes as one of the more intriguing gameplan storylines of 2026.
Since joining the Warriors from Penrith in 2020, Egan has become the club’s most-capped hooker (105 games, plus 15 appearances from the bench and one at five-eighth) and developed into arguably our best ever to play the position.
Egan was one of the most important cogs of the Warriors’ watershed 2023 campaign and emerged as a candidate for a NSW Origin debut courtesy of some top-shelf performances in early-2024. That season, however, was ultimately most remembered for a spate of early HIA exits and a troublesome elbow injury.

### PROPS
Headlined by two experienced, elite internationals and a contender for the position’s best young prospect in the NRL, prop is arguably the Warriors’ greatest area of strength and depth at present.
Beyond co-captains James Fisher-Harris and Mitch Barnett, and 21-year-old weapon Demitric Vaimauga, however, the front-row pecking order is a fascinating part of the Warriors’ 2026 squad balance.
Kangaroos incumbent Barnett’s early-2025 form was enough to earn a starting spot for NSW in the Origin opener, but an ACL injury soon afterwards in a win over Souths ruled him out of the rest of the series – and the Warriors’ season.
Initially, the Warriors coped superbly without the 31-year-old hardman, whipping Cronulla – and a pack led by former Warriors cornerstone Addin Fonua-Blake – 40-10.

### BACK-ROWERS
Completing our pre-season positional preview series, the Warriors’ burgeoning contingent of second-rowers and locks.
The club is flush with some of the most eye-watering back-row talent in the NRL, as well as the reigning Dally M Lock of the Year, a current Queensland Origin rep and a former Kiwi in the veteran class, and a versatile young Super League import looking to squeeze his way into the Warriors’ line-up.
Erin Clark was widely regarded as the NRL’s unofficial buy of the year in 2025. He earned a spot in the Dally M Team of the Year, finished a vote outside the top 20 in the Dally M Medal count and was surprisingly overlooked for the Simon Mannering Medal. The nuggetty hooker-turned-lock was, however, the 2025 TWL Kingz Container Crew Player of the Year, which rewards season-long excellence.

## SEASON SUMMARIES (key facts only)
1995: DEBUT SEASON. Finished 10th (22 games). Crowd avg 26,632. Phil Blake scored first-ever try Rd1 v Broncos. Lost 25-22. Dean Bell captain.
1996: First full 24-game ARL season. Finished 9th.
1997: Super League season. Frank Endacott replaced John Monie mid-season.
1998: First NRL season. Crowd avg 8,858 (lowest ever). Endacott sacked end-year.
1999: Mark Graham era began. First finals appearance — eliminated week 1. Stacey Jones 15 tries (top scorer).
2001: First finals series under Daniel Anderson. Stacey Jones & Kevin Campion co-captains. Anderson won Dally M CoY.
2002: GRAND FINAL YEAR. Went to Sydney Roosters 30-8. Minor premiers. Ivan Cleary scored 242 pts (season record). Warriors' 11 players in a record Kiwis win 30-16 over Australia.
2003: Finals series. Francis Meli 23 tries (then-record). Meli scored 5 tries v Bulldogs in finals (NRL finals record). Jones 4 field goals in season.
2007: Top 4 finish (only 2nd time in history at that point). Grant Rovelli hat-trick v Titans in Aug. Finals exits: Eels then Cowboys.
2008: Five Warriors in Kiwis 2008 World Cup final team — NZ beat Australia 34-20. Manu Vatuvei key. Steve Price and Ruben Wiki era.
2011: GRAND FINAL YEAR. Lost to Manly 24-10. Shaun Johnson debut season (his rookie of the year). Lewis Brown try sent Warriors to GF. Biggest home crowd ever: 38,412 (Eden Park, Rd1).
2018: Return to finals (10-yr drought ended). RTS won Dally M Medal (Warriors' first ever). Blake Green/SJ combination. Won 17 of 24 games. Crowd avg 17,376 (Mt Smart only).
2019: Shaun Johnson released (signed Cronulla). Ken Maumalo 17 tries. Roger Tuivasa-Sheck won TWL POTY, club POTY for 3rd straight year.
2020: COVID ODYSSEY. Based in Australia 20 games. Finished 10th. Warriors created history: first successful Captain's Challenge. Adam Blair oldest Warrior in 12+ years (34y 191d). Todd Payten caretaker.
2021: Still in Australia. Josh Curran, Reece Walsh standouts (Walsh: 9 tries, 78 pts as rookie — most pts by a Warriors rookie). Jazz Tevaga won TWL POTY. Nathan Brown coach.
2022: 70-10 loss to Storm (biggest loss ever). DWZ 9 tries. Stacey Jones last 11 games as interim coach after Brown departure.
2023: WATERSHED SEASON under Webster. Top 4 (3rd), preliminary final (lost Broncos 22-18, crowd 52,273). 19 wins regular season. SJ won Dally M HB of Year (robbed of Medal). DWZ 24 tries (new record). SJ won TWL POTY (avg 7.84 — season record). Season avg crowd 22,685.
2024: Finals week 1 exit v Penrith. First EVERY home game sold out. Fonua-Blake & Barnett TWL POTY leaders. Barnett debuted for NSW (first Warrior) and Australia (2nd ever, after Villasanti 2003). 66-6 loss to Titans (equal-biggest margin). Roger RTS returned from rugby union.
2025: Co-captains JFH & Barnett. Tohu Harris medically retired (back). 10-3 start (best in club history). Luke Metcalf ACL (Rd15). Barnett ACL (mid-season). Erin Clark emerged (147m/game, 34.3 tkl/game). Leka Halasima 13 tries (shortlisted Dally M Rookie). 14 wins, finals week 1 exit v Penrith 24-8. Avg crowd 25,382 (all-time high).
